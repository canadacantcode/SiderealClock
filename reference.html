Reference & Sources

This page lists the main references we used to build our Mars and Earth sidereal and solar time clocks, along with background on Karl Jansky and radio astronomy.

Time & Astronomy References

  • Greenwich Mean Sidereal Time (GMST) formulas and J2000 epoch definitions from standard astronomy references and IAU conventions.
  • Mars Sol Date (MSD) and Mars Coordinated Time (MTC) based on published Mars timekeeping descriptions used by NASA mission teams.
  • Concepts of solar vs. sidereal day for both Earth and Mars from introductory astronomy textbooks.

Karl Jansky & Radio Astronomy

  • Historical information about Karl Jansky’s rotating antenna at Bell Telephone Laboratories in Holmdel, NJ, and the discovery of radio noise from the direction of the Milky Way’s center.
  • Details on how Jansky noticed the signal repeating on a sidereal, not solar, schedule — with Bell Labs engineer Marvin Skellett pointing out that the cycle matched sidereal time.
  • Background on how this discovery led to the birth of radio astronomy and later work on quasars, radio galaxies, and the cosmic microwave background.

Our Own Work

  • Clock implementation and layout designed and coded by us, using simplified formulas to make the sidereal and solar times easy to visualize.
  • We are currently working on adding 2D visual models and more accurate timing corrections (like Mars’s elliptical orbit and improved timing data).

How the Site Is Built

  • Static site hosted with GitHub Pages, written in plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
  • Parts of the layout and structure were assisted by an AI helper, but the astronomical research, equations, and final time logic were developed by us.
