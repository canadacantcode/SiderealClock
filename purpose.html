Our Purpose

This site is inspired by Karl Jansky, often called the father of radio astronomy. By studying strange signals with a rotating radio antenna, he accidentally discovered radio waves coming from the Milky Way.

Why we care about sidereal time

Jansky noticed that the “radio noise” he measured did not repeat every 24 hours. Instead, it followed a sidereal day, which is slightly shorter and based on the stars. This pattern showed that the signal was coming from space, not Earth.

  • Sidereal time repeats when the sky looks the same again.
  • It helped Jansky realize the source was the Milky Way.

How this connects to Mars

Our Mars and Earth clocks show both solar and sidereal time, similar to how Jansky compared the daily cycle of his antenna signal. For Mars, this helps us imagine how future astronomers might track the sky from another planet.

  • Solar time follows the Sun in the sky.
  • Sidereal time follows the background stars.

Big idea

By visualizing these different clocks, we highlight how careful timing and rotation measurements led Jansky to open an entirely new window on the universe: radio astronomy.
