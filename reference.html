References & Sources

The following sources were used to research sidereal and solar time on Earth and Mars, as well as the historical context of Karl Jansky’s discovery and the origins of radio astronomy.

Historical Articles

Cosmic Search Vol. 1, No. 4 — “My Brother Karl Jansky”

A first-hand historical account describing Karl Jansky’s work, his early experiments, and the significance of discovering radio waves from the Milky Way.

Websites

Sidereal Time — Wikipedia

Used to understand the definition of sidereal time and how Earth’s rotation relative to distant stars differs from solar time.

NASA Mars24 Algorithm Documentation

Primary technical reference for Mars Sol Date (MSD), Mars Coordinated Time (MTC), and the mathematical framework behind Martian timekeeping.

MarsTI — Mars Time FAQ

Provided inspiration and alternative explanations for Mars time concepts, including sols, Mars clocks, and public-facing Mars time displays.

Julian Day — Wikipedia

Used to understand Julian Date conventions and why they are commonly used as a continuous time reference in astronomy and space science.

U.S. Naval Observatory — Sidereal Time

Authoritative astronomical reference explaining sidereal time calculations and their use in precise Earth rotation measurements.
